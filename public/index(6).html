FAQ

Things people actually ask.

Is this really free? +
Yes. CaptionMojo is in free public beta. You get a limited number of AI generations per day per browser — enough to genuinely build a library and test the tool. No card, no signup, no time limit on the beta. Paid plans with accounts, larger limits, and team features are coming soon.
What information should I have ready before setting up? +
The more specific you are, the better your captions. Before you start, think about: who your ideal customer is (age, lifestyle, what they care about), what makes you different from competitors, how you'd describe your brand voice in three words, and any phrases you'd never use. You don't need all of this for Quick Start — but having it ready makes Deep Setup very fast.
What's the difference between Quick and Deep setup? +
Quick asks five plain questions and gets you generating in 30 seconds. Perfect for getting a feel for the tool. Deep asks for more detail across eight steps — full audience profile, platform-specific rules, USP, document uploads, and real caption examples. Both work. Deep gives the AI more to work with, so the output sits closer to your real brand voice.
What documents can I upload? +
You can upload PDF, Word, TXT, Markdown, and image files. The AI reads them all alongside your profile answers. What makes the biggest difference: brand identity docs (origin story, full product/service catalog, taglines), audience descriptions, brand voice guides, real copy examples (your best captions, captions from accounts whose voice you want to match), positioning and competitor notes, and any cultural references or inside knowledge specific to your brand. Don't have formal documents? Open a notes app, paste everything you know about your brand, upload it as one file. A thorough brain dump beats a thin style guide every time.
What are audience segments and how does the AI build them? +
Audience segments are the specific groups of people you post for — not generic categories but real slices of your audience. A restaurant might have "weekend brunch crowd," "private events inquiries," and "weekday regulars." A fitness studio might have "new members," "competitive athletes," and "over-40 wellness crowd." The AI reads your brand profile and suggests segments that make sense for your specific business. You review every one, approve the ones you want, rename them to match your language, and add any the AI missed.
How do I export captions to use elsewhere? +
In the Folders tab, go to any folder (Final, In Progress, Liked) and tap Export .txt or Copy All. Export downloads a clean text file organized by segment and platform — ready to paste into a Google Doc, Notion, Airtable, your scheduling tool, or hand directly to a team member. Copy All puts everything on your clipboard at once.
How do I get the best captions from the AI? +
Three things make the biggest difference. First, be specific in your brand profile — "warm and friendly" is useful but "sounds like a knowledgeable friend, short sentences, never salesy, uses the word 'real' a lot" is much better. Second, paste real example captions you love in Deep Setup — this is the highest-value input you can give. Third, use the Direction field when generating — a specific brief like "new summer menu launch" or "for the customer who just discovered us" produces far better output than leaving it blank.
Is my brand information private? +
Yes. During the beta, your brand profile, segments, and caption library are stored locally in your browser — not on our servers. Nothing is sent to other users, ever. When AI generation happens, your brand profile is included so Claude can calibrate the output — but it's used only for that request and isn't used to train any models. When we add accounts in a future release, we'll make data handling explicit and give you control over what's stored.
What's the difference between the workflow folders? +
Liked is your personal shortlist — captions you want to remember but haven't decided on yet. In Progress is for captions that need editing before they're ready. Final is your approved, post-ready library — this is what you export. Trash holds discarded captions so nothing is permanently deleted until you choose to empty it. You can move any caption between folders at any time.
When will accounts and paid plans launch? +
We're shipping the beta first so we can learn what people actually want before we lock features behind subscriptions. Accounts, team workspaces, larger generation limits, and paid plans are next on the roadmap. If you want to be told when they're live, use the tool today — when accounts ship, we'll let beta users know.
